Trying to add betting to my Blackjack assignment ate up a lot of time before i gave up and handed in "No-Stakes Blackjack". Returning to the game after graduating by the skin of my teeth, I made multiple attempts to add bank & betting on top of the 'finished' product, only to have it wreak havoc on scoring and layout. Subsequent attempts at debugging just made a bigger mess (much like Homer visiting the past in Treehouse of Horror.)

I realized if I was going to figure out betting logic, it had to be isolated from the complexity of potential Blackjack outcomes. I went with the simplest game of chance possible - a coin flip - and focused on the logic of "guess right, add money, guess wrong, subtract money."

Taking it back to basics helped me better understand the separation of interests in game logic - in an earlier attempt, i was defining all 5 possible Blackjack outcomes, adding or subtracting from the bank, and updating the DOM all within the same function. Of course I couldn't change one thing without breaking another!

Even in this little 2-outcome game, my code has room for improvement. For example, I ended up defining two separate callback functions (confirmHeads and confirmTails) rather than using event delegation to simply 'confirmUserChoice'. With only two outcomes, it seemed like more work than it was worth, but in future projects, I'll need a better understanding of event delegation.
